HA NOI

Hanoi, the thousand-year-old capital of Vietnam, is the country's most important cultural, political, and economic center. Known for its timeless beauty and rich history, the city preserves numerous famous landmarks such as Hoan Kiem Lake, the Temple of Literature, the Imperial Citadel of Thang Long, along with the bustling Old Quarter. Beyond its traditional values, Hanoi is also a hub of modern development, with new urban areas, shopping centers, and an expanding transportation network.

Visitors to Hanoi cannot miss its unique cuisine, featuring renowned dishes like Pho, Bun cha, or Banh Cuon, while also enjoying the serene and peaceful atmosphere of lakes and green parks scattered throughout the city.

UNMISSABLE ATTRACTIONS
TEMPLE OF LITERATURE
The Temple of Literature, built in 1070 during the Ly Dynasty, is Vietnam's first university and a symbol of its educational and cultural heritage. Dedicated to Confucius, it features serene courtyards, ancient architecture, and stone steles honoring scholars, reflecting Vietnam's long-standing tradition of learning.
Adress: Temple of Literature - Quoc Tu Giam, 58 Quoc Tu Giam Street, Dong Da District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
HOAN KIEM LAKE
Hoan Kiem Lake, in central Hanoi, is an iconic symbol of peace and harmony. Surrounded by ancient trees, it offers a serene escape from city life. Named after the legend of the Golden Turtle and the restored sword, the area features Ngoc Son Temple and The Huc Bridge, adding to its historical appeal, making it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.
Adress: Hoan Kiem Lake, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
HO CHI MINH MAUSOLEUM
The Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum in Hanoi houses the embalmed body of President Ho Chi Minh, founder of modern Vietnam. Located at Ba Dinh Square, where he declared independence in 1945, it combines Vietnamese and Soviet architecture, symbolizing deep respect and gratitude from the Vietnamese people.
Adress: Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, 2 Hung Vuong Street, Ba Dinh District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
HOA LO PRISON
Hoa Lo Prison, also known as the "Hanoi Hilton," is a historic site in Hanoi. Originally built by the French in 1896 to detain Vietnamese political prisoners, it was later used to hold American POWs during the Vietnam War. Now a museum, it preserves artifacts and exhibits, reflecting Vietnam's struggle for independence.
Adress: Hoa Lo Prison, 1 Hoa Lo Street, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
HANOI OLD QUARTED
Hanoi's Old Quarter is a vibrant and bustling area known for its narrow streets, rich history, and well-preserved colonial architecture. Each street in the Old Quarter is traditionally associated with a specific trade, giving it the nickname 'The 36 Streets.' Visitors can explore local markets, sample authentic street food, and admire ancient temples and pagodas nestled between modern shops. The Old Quarter is a unique blend of old and new, offering a glimpse into the traditional lifestyle of Hanoi while showcasing the city's dynamic energy.
Adress: Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem District, Hanoi, Vietnam.
