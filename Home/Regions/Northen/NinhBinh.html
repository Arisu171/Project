NINH BINH

Ninh Bình is a captivating province in northern Vietnam, celebrated for its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Often referred to as the “Halong Bay on Land,” Ninh Bình features dramatic limestone karsts, serene rivers, and lush rice paddies. The province is home to the famous Trang An Scenic Landscape Complex and the ancient capital of Hoa Lu, offering visitors a glimpse into Vietnam's historical past. With its picturesque caves, temples, and traditional villages, Ninh Bình provides a tranquil escape from the bustling city life, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and history enthusiasts alike.

UNMISSABLE ATTRACTIONS
Trang an
Trang An, located in Ninh Binh province, is renowned for its limestone formations, winding rivers, and lush greenery. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it offers a unique boat tour through picturesque caves and waterways. The area is also known for its historical temples and pagodas amidst stunning scenery, providing a serene journey through one of Vietnam’s most breathtaking landscapes.
Adress: Trang An, Ninh Binh, Vietnam.
Tam coc - bich dong
Tam Coc - Bich Dong, known as the "Halong Bay on Land," is a stunning natural attraction in Ninh Binh. It features scenic river valleys, limestone peaks, and lush rice paddies. Boat rides along the Ngo Dong River take visitors through a landscape of temples, caves, and the hillside Bich Dong Pagoda, offering panoramic views.
Adress: Tam Coc, Ninh Binh, Vietnam.
chua bai dinh
Bai Dinh Pagoda, located in Ninh Binh, is the largest complex of Buddhist temples in Vietnam. Known for its impressive architecture, it features a colossal bronze Buddha and beautifully landscaped gardens. Visitors can explore its halls, towers, and enjoy views of the countryside, experiencing the rich heritage of Vietnamese Buddhism.
Adress: Bai Dinh, Gia Sinh, Gia Vien, Ninh Binh, Vietnam.
