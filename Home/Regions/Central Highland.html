Central Highlands

DISCOVER CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

The Central Highlands, is a captivating region in Vietnam known for its rugged landscapes, diverse ethnic cultures, and rich natural resources. Characterized by its rolling plateaus, vast coffee plantations, and majestic waterfalls, this region offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore vibrant local markets, traditional villages, and scenic lakes, such as Lake Tuyen Lam and Lake Bien Ho. The Central Highlands is also renowned for its cool climate and adventurous outdoor activities, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts alike.

SNAPSHOTS OF CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

The Central Highlands, or Tay Nguyen, is a stunning region with rolling hills, coffee plantations, and majestic waterfalls like Datanla. Its cool climate, ethnic villages, and vibrant festivals showcase rich cultural traditions, making it ideal for nature lovers seeking a peaceful escape.

TASTE OF CENTRAL HIGHLANDS

Central Highlands cuisine blends fresh, local ingredients into hearty dishes like grilled meats, stews, and rice. Specialties include “com lam” (bamboo-cooked rice) and “bo nuong la lot” (grilled beef in betel leaves), offering a taste of the region's rich culinary traditions.k

Com Lam

Com lam is a traditional dish from Vietnam's Central Highlands, known for its unique preparation and distinct flavor. This specialty involves cooking rice inside bamboo tubes over an open fire, which imparts a subtle, smoky aroma to the rice. The result is a fragrant, slightly nutty-flavored rice that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, stews, or pickled vegetables. Com lam reflects the region’s resourceful cooking techniques and is a cherished part of Central Highlands cuisine.

Pho Kho Gia Lai

Pho Kho Gia Lai is a unique and beloved dish from the Central Highlands of Vietnam, specifically Gia Lai Province. Unlike traditional pho, this dry noodle dish features rice noodles served without broth, accompanied by a savory mix of tender beef, crispy fried shallots, fresh herbs, and a flavorful sauce. The dish is often enjoyed with pickled vegetables and fresh chili, offering a delightful combination of textures and flavors. Pho Kho Gia Lai is a testament to the region’s rich culinary heritage and is a must-try for those exploring the food of Tay Nguyen.

Grilled Chicken

Grilled Chicken is a renowned specialty of the Central Highlands, celebrated for its distinctive flavor and preparation. This dish features tender chicken marinated in a blend of local spices and herbs, then grilled to perfection over an open flame. The result is a deliciously smoky and savory chicken with a crispy exterior and juicy interior. Traditionally served with a variety of fresh vegetables and dipping sauces, Grilled Chicken is a beloved culinary delight that captures the essence of Central Highlands cuisine.

Civet Coffee

Civet coffee, known as “ca phe chon” in Vietnamese, is a unique and highly sought-after coffee variety from the Central Highlands. This specialty coffee is made using beans that have been eaten and then excreted by civet cats. The digestive enzymes of the civet enhance the coffee’s flavor, resulting in a smooth, rich, and less acidic brew. With its distinctive production process and refined taste, civet coffee is considered a luxury item and a fascinating part of the Central Highlands' coffee culture.
