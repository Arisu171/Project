QUANG BINH

Quang Binh, a province located in Central Vietnam, is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Home to the famous Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Quang Binh boasts some of the world’s most impressive caves, including Son Doong, the largest cave in the world. The province also features beautiful beaches, historical sites, and picturesque mountain scenery.

Quang Binh’s diverse attractions and tranquil environment make it an ideal destination for adventure seekers and those looking to experience Vietnam’s natural wonders.

UNMISSABLE ATTRACTIONS
Phong Nha - Ke Bang
Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Quang Binh Province, is famous for its karst landscapes and extensive cave systems. Spanning over 800,000 hectares, it includes the world’s largest cave, Son Doong. Visitors can explore limestone formations, underground rivers, and lush forests, making it a top destination for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers.
Adress: Bo Trach District, Quang Binh Province.
En Cave
En Cave, located in Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park, is the third largest cave in the world by volume. Known for its impressive size, dramatic rock formations, and unique ecosystem, it offers thrilling trekking and caving adventures. Visitors can explore vast chambers, underground rivers, and lush vegetation, experiencing the grandeur of Vietnam’s cave systems.
Adress: Bo Trach District, Quang Binh Province, Vietnam.
Nhat Le Beach
Nhat Le Beach, in Quang Binh Province, is a picturesque destination known for its golden sand and gentle waves. With stunning views of the East Sea, it offers a tranquil spot for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the sea breeze. The beach is also popular for its fresh seafood and charming promenades, providing a serene escape along Vietnam’s coastline.
Adress: Dong Hoi City, Quang Binh Province, Vietnam.
Ngang Pass
Ngang Pass, also known as Hoanh Son Quan, is a historic mountain pass in Quang Binh Province. Famous for its breathtaking views and winding roads, it offers stunning vistas of lush landscapes and the coastline. A key route for centuries, it connects northern and central Vietnam. Visitors can enjoy the natural beauty and cool highland breeze while exploring this iconic pass.
Adress: Quang Binh Province, Vietnam.
