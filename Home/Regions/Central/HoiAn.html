HOI AN

Hoi An is a charming ancient town known for its well-preserved architecture, traditional culture, and vibrant street markets. Once a bustling trading port, the town now attracts visitors with its beautiful lantern-lit streets, historic buildings, and scenic riverfront. Strolling through the narrow lanes, visitors can explore ancient temples, wooden shop-houses, and centuries-old bridges.

Hoi An is also famous for its delicious local cuisine and skilled tailors, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking history, culture, and craft.

UNMISSABLE ATTRACTIONS
pho co hoi an
Hoi An Ancient Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is known for its preserved historic charm and architectural beauty. The town reflects Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese, and European influences. Visitors can explore ancient houses, canals, and markets, while the streets come alive at night with colorful lanterns. Hoi An offers a unique glimpse into Vietnam's rich history.
Adress: Hoi An, Quang Nam, Vietnam.
Thu Bon River
The Thu Bon River flows through Hoi An, offering a tranquil escape from the bustling town. Vital to the local ecosystem and culture, it serves as a means of transport and livelihood. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, scenic views, and traditional riverside villages. The river enhances Hoi An's serene charm, perfect for relaxation and exploration.
Adress: Hoi An, Quang Nam, Vietnam.
chua cau
The Japanese Covered Bridge, or Chua Cau, is an iconic symbol of Hoi An, built in the 16th century by Japanese merchants. It blends Japanese, Chinese, and Vietnamese architectural styles, with a wooden structure, intricate carvings, and a small temple. This picturesque bridge spans a canal and reflects Hoi An's rich cultural heritage.
Adress: Hoi An, Quang Nam, Vietnam.
lang gom thanh ha
Thanh Ha Pottery Village, near Hoi An, is known for its high-quality ceramics, crafted for centuries. Visitors can watch skilled artisans create pottery, from daily items to intricate artworks, using time-honored techniques. The village offers a glimpse into traditional pottery-making and a chance to purchase authentic, handcrafted pieces.
Adress: Thanh Ha, Hoi An, Quang Nam, Vietnam.
