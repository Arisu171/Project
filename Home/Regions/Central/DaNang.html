Da Nang

Da Nang, a vibrant coastal city in Central Vietnam, is known for its beautiful beaches, modern infrastructure, and proximity to cultural landmarks. As a gateway to UNESCO World Heritage Sites like Hoi An and My Son, Da Nang has become a popular destination for both local and international travelers. The city is home to the famous Marble Mountains, Dragon Bridge, and the stunning My Khe Beach.

With a perfect blend of natural beauty and urban charm, Da Nang offers a dynamic and welcoming experience for visitors.

UNMISSABLE ATTRACTIONS
Ba Na Hills
Ba Na Hills, located just outside Da Nang, is a popular mountain resort known for its cool climate, scenic views, and unique attractions. Perched at an elevation of over 1,400 meters, the area is home to the famous Golden Bridge, a stunning structure held up by giant stone hands. Ba Na Hills also features a European-style village, beautiful gardens, and one of the world’s longest cable cars, offering breathtaking panoramas of the surrounding landscape. It’s a must-visit destination for those looking to escape the city and enjoy a blend of natural beauty and entertainment.
Adress: Hoa Ninh Commune, Hoa Vang District.
My Khe Beach
My Khe Beach, one of Da Nang’s most famous beaches, is known for its soft white sand, gentle waves, and clear blue waters. Stretching for several kilometers, it's ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports like surfing. With stunning ocean views and a calm atmosphere, My Khe is a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.
Adress: Da Nang City, Vietnam.
Dragon Bridge
Dragon Bridge, one of Da Nang's most iconic landmarks, spans the Han River with a design resembling a dragon, symbolizing power and prosperity. The bridge features a nightly light show where the dragon’s head breathes fire and water, creating a stunning display. Opened in 2013, it symbolizes the city's modern growth and is a must-see attraction.
Adress: An Hai Tay, Son Tra District, Da Nang, Vietnam.
Son Tra Peninsula
Son Tra Peninsula, just outside Da Nang, is a natural wonder known for its lush forests, diverse wildlife, and stunning coastal views. Called the "green lung" of the city, it features scenic trails, the iconic Linh Ung Pagoda with its Lady Buddha statue, and sightings of rare red-shanked douc langurs. A perfect spot for nature lovers, it combines tranquility with breathtaking landscapes.
Adress: Tho Quang, Son Tra District, Da Nang, Vietnam.
Marble Mountains
The Marble Mountains, just south of Da Nang, are a group of five limestone hills named after the elements—Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, and Earth. Known for their natural beauty and historical significance, they feature caves, temples, and pagodas, along with stunning summit views. The area is also famous for its marble sculptures, making it a must-visit for those exploring Da Nang.
Adress: Hoa Hai, Ngu Hanh Son District, Da Nang, Vietnam.
