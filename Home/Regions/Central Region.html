CENTRAL REGION

DISCOVER CENTRAL REGION

Central Region Vietnam, is a region known for its diverse landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance. Stretching along the coast, it offers stunning beaches, dramatic mountain ranges, and ancient landmarks such as the Imperial City of Huế and the ancient town of Hoi An. The region is also famous for its distinctive cuisine, featuring spicy and flavorful dishes. Central Vietnam is a captivating destination, blending natural beauty with deep cultural roots.

SNAPSHOTS OF CENTRAL REGION

Central Vietnam boasts stunning beaches, mountains, and cultural sites. Highlights include Da Nang, Hai Van Pass, Phong Nha-Ke Bang, Hoi An, and Hue.

TASTE OF CENTRAL REGION

Central Vietnam's cuisine is bold, blending spicy, salty, and sour flavors. Seafood dishes like “bun bo Hue,” “mi Quang,” and fresh spring rolls are known for their flavor and artistry.

Bun Bo Hue

Bun Bo Hue is a signature dish from the city of Hue, located in Central Vietnam. Known for its rich and flavorful broth, this spicy beef noodle soup combines tender slices of beef, pork, and thick rice noodles. The broth is infused with lemongrass, chili, and shrimp paste, creating a complex and aromatic taste. Often served with fresh herbs, lime, and chili, Bun Bo Hue is a beloved dish that reflects the culinary heritage of Central Vietnam and is a must-try for food lovers.

Mi Quang

Mi Quang is a beloved dish from Central Vietnam, known for its distinct flavors and vibrant presentation. This noodle dish is made with wide rice noodles, topped with a rich broth, tender meats such as pork, shrimp, or chicken, and garnished with fresh herbs, roasted peanuts, and crispy rice crackers. Unlike traditional Vietnamese soups, Mi Quang has just enough broth to coat the noodles, giving it a unique texture and taste. It’s a culinary highlight of Central Vietnam and a must-try for visitors exploring the region’s diverse food culture.

Nem Nuong Nha Trang

Nem Nuong Nha Trang is a famous grilled pork dish from the coastal city of Nha Trang, located in Central Vietnam. This dish features flavorful, marinated pork skewers grilled to perfection and served with fresh vegetables, herbs, rice paper, and a rich, savory dipping sauce. The balance of smoky, savory meat and the freshness of the accompanying ingredients makes Nem Nuong Nha Trang a beloved culinary specialty, offering a taste of the region's vibrant street food culture.

Eel Soup

Nghe An Eel Soup is a beloved specialty of Central Vietnam, known for its rich, savory flavor and aromatic spices. Made with tender eel meat, the dish is simmered in a flavorful broth infused with lemongrass, turmeric, and chili, creating a comforting and hearty soup. Served with crispy fried shallots and fresh herbs, this traditional dish showcases the bold flavors of Nghe An’s culinary heritage. Eel soup is a must-try for visitors looking to experience the authentic tastes of Central Vietnam.
