Exploring Hanoi: A Journey through the 36 Ancient Streets and Unmissable Local Dishes

Hoa Nguyen

12 Oct 2024 - 13 min read

Introduction:

Hanoi, the heart of Vietnam, is famous for its 36 ancient streets, narrow alleys full of culture, and rich history. A day spent wandering around the Old Quarter allows you to fully experience the charm of the capital.

Personal Experience:

During my trip, Hanoi appeared peaceful through early morning strolls around Hoan Kiem Lake, observing the slow-paced life of the locals. I visited Ngoc Son Temple, the Temple of Literature, and of course indulged in famous dishes like Pho Bat Dan, Bun Cha Hang Manh, and Banh Cuon Thanh Van-each with its own unique and unforgettable flavor.

Travel Tip:

To truly enjoy the Old Quarter, visit early in the morning when the air is fresh and the streets are calm. In the evening, the Dong Xuan market and street food vendors come alive-perfect for a local food experience.

Review:

Hanoi is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves a mix of history, culture, and rich cuisine. The city is steeped in history, with landmarks like the ancient Temple of Literature, Vietnam's first university, showcasing the country’s scholarly traditions. The majestic Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum stands as a tribute to the nation’s founding father, while the bustling streets tell stories of the past through their colonial architecture and time-worn facades. Each site is a portal to the fascinating history that has shaped Vietnam's identity
